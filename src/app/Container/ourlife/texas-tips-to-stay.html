Texas Tips to Stay Cool on a Budget
  • Our Energy Llc
  • Energy Tips Our Energy Buzz Texas
  • No comments

In Texas seasons work differently than the rest of the country.  Here it can get: dry, arid, muggy, sticky, hot and “hotter than Hades”.  It’s also one of the few places where you’ll wear a jacket one day and be scorched by blistering heat the next.  Still, no one can take away the fact that we are better at enjoying summer than anyone else.  We’re experts at making the best out of hot weather!  Here are 10 Texas Tips to Stay Cool on a Budget.

15 Texas Tips to Stay Cool on a Budget

TIP #1:  TEXAS STYLE

Tight, heavy clothes become unbearable in the heat.  Consider wearing loose fitting outfits with breathable fabrics to comfortable.  So you don’t have a pair of shorts on hand?  No problem!  Cut off shorts are all the range these days.  Don’t forget your hat!

TIP #2:  DRINK PLENTY OF WATER

You can go weeks without food but only a few days without water.  According to LiveScience, once you are thirsty, you’re dehydrated and have already lost 1% of your body water.  Losing body water can lead to heat stroke and even death.  Keeping hydrated will ensure that you keep yourself cool from the inside out.  Drink up Texas!

TIP #3:  ICED TEA

Does your iced water seem to simple to quench your thirst?  You must be a Texan!  We can’t think of summertime without enjoy a delicious glass of ice cold sweet tea.  Never treat a Texan to iced tea that comes out of a can.  We’ve made a science out of brewing delightful sweet tea with lovely variations such as cinnamon and orange rinds.  It’s the perfect treat for a pool party.  Looking for something with a little more of a kick?  On a hot weekend night, nothing will be better than a Texas iced tea!  Cheers to that!

TIP #4:  TAKE A DIP IN THE POOL

Cool off from the outside!  Whether it’s a big fancy pool, a water park, a kiddie pool, a makeshift pool or a cold shower enjoy it!  Submerging yourself in cold water on a hot day is beneficial on many levels.  Cool water will ease stress, help with depression, is great for the hair and skin, boosts the immune system and will cool you off.  Cannonball anyone?

TIP #5:  TEXAS TUBBING

What can be better than taking a dip in the pool?  Let’s head over to the river!  Grab a tube and a cold beer and enjoy floating down the river.  Whether it’s floating, swimming, diving, kayaking, boating or just sitting enjoy!  A hot day is the best excuse to enjoy one mother nature’s finest Texas tips to stay cool.

TIP #6:  FRESH FRUIT AND VEGGIES

Keep from sinking while tubbing!  Although we absolutely love BBQ’s, it’s a great idea to eat your greens.  The heavier the food, the more your body will heat up.  It’s effort to burn those calories will make you feel sluggish and hot.  By munching on salads as well as fresh fruit, you will keep energized and light.  You can still enjoy your brisket, just make sure you also add a little salad and fruit.  Bring out the watermelon and ruby reds!

TIP #7:  GUACAMOLE AND CHIPS

Now that you are wondering about what vegetables to add to your BBQ for the next pool party or trip to the river…  Here’s one of the  yummiest Texas tips to stay cool on a budget.  Guacamole and chips are a great way to go!  Here’s a delightful Texas favorite that requires zero cooking, keeping your energy bill low.  Avocados are high in fiber, low in fat, highly nutritious and incredibly delicious.  Whip yourself up a great batch of guacamole and keep it from turning brown by leaving a seed or two in the batch.  Let it chill a bit in the fridge and it will help cool you off and energize you during those hot days.  Dig in!

TIP #8:  DON’T FORGET THE FANS

Fans are awesome for both energy efficiency and decoration.  Ceiling fans can be placed to be used alone or to help circulate cool air from the air conditioner.  Modern fans come in all colors, shapes and sizes and can be very quiet.  Make sure that the fan is set to turn counterclockwise to bring the cool air down.  This will help save you up to 40% of your air conditioning costs!  Outdoor fans or fans over a table are useful to keep the bugs away from the food and yourself.  Y’all be so happy, y’all sing, “You spin me right round, baby. Right round like a fan, baby!”

TIP #9:  THE TEXAS COOL

Now that you’ve got the fans, you can practice the “Texas Cool”!  This tip from This Old House is very easy and can save you “from 20 to 50% off your cooling bill”.  Trap the cool night air into your home by placing window fans by open windows.  Then, in the daytime close your windows and shades to keep the cool air in.  Now this is one of the best Texas Tips to Stay Cool on a Budget!  But wait!  The best is yet to come!…

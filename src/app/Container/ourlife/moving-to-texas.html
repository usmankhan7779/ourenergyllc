Moving to Texas

Let us give you a big, warm Texas-style welcome! We are always happy about a new addition to the Texas community! Home of the big city, big country, big football and big hearted folk.

On October of 2013, Time Magazine featured the cover article, “ The United States of Texas”. The article mainly discussed why the Lone Star State is considered “America’s Future”. Way before this article was published, Texans always knew that this is grand place to live in. After conducting several interviews and much reading, the following are my top reasons why if you’re not living in Texas, you should consider moving here:

  •   It’s a Great Place to Set up Shop.

    If you are a business owner, Texas has great tax incentives for businesses. In addition, Texas is not big on unions and has a excellent workforce to choose from.

  • It’s Where the Jobs Are.

    With so many businesses popping up, there is a wealth of jobs. From blue collar to white collar jobs, there are many opportunities in a broad range of industries.

  • No State Income Tax.

    Lower taxes, need I say more? It’s what we all want.

  • Friendly and Down to Earth Crowd.

    The Texans are known for their Southern hospitality and direct approach. If you still believe in good manners and just being nice, you’ll feel right at home.

  • More Bang for your Buck.

    With taxes, property, utilities and transportation costs being lower, your paycheck will go much further. It’s called quality of life, there’s much more of it out here.

  • Warmer Weather.

    Although we will occasionally see some snow during the winter, overall Texas enjoys milder winters than their neighbors to the North. If you have any experience ploying snow or dusting off your car in the morning, it will be a welcome change.

  • Rich Cultural Diversity.

    The legendary cowboy way is still much alive in Texas but that’s not the only one. Texas is bustling with a rich mix of multiple cultures due to the great influx of immigrants from all over. This equates to great food, culture and a wide variety of experiences.

  • The Food.

    I have to once again mention the food. You’ve never really lived until you savor a big all out Texan barbecue, a breakfast burrito or a spicy Tex-Mex delight. Frozen tamarind margaritas, anyone?

  • A Great Place to Raise a Family.

    Texas believes in traditional family values. It’s a state with a young population and good schools.

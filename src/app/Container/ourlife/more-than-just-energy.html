The largest city in Texas as well as Southern United States, Houston is simply a hit. If you have been looking for more than just energy companies in Houston, we invite you to get to know more about one of the most productive cities in the U.S. and it’s inhabitants.

More than Just Energy Companies in Houston:  Why Houston is a Hit

Here are a few interesting facts about Houston and why so many people just love to live here:

What’s in a Name?

Houston has several official and unofficial nicknames. It was baptized Space City officially in 1967 as the home of NASA’s Manned Spacecraft Center.   Yet, H-Town is a city of many faces, here are a few:

– Bayou City: Ten lovely waterways not only provided a nickname but more importantly help give life to its many city parks.

– Hustle Town: A hip-hop modification of the popular H-Town.

– The Energy Capital of the World: Houston’s unmatched domination of nearly every segment of the energy industry earned them the title and is the trigger behind the city’s sprawling growth.

Source: Wikipedia

– THE BIG HEART. We are tough on the outside but have a huge heart on the inside! Nothing says more about how much Houstonians care like how they took in more than 150,000 survivors of Hurricane Katrina.

No Politics During Dinner Please.

If you are ever thinking of visiting a friend in Houston, it’s best to keep the focus on family, sports, and the weather, anything other than politics. One third of the people prefer not to chitchat about politics with friends or family. According to the Center for Houston’s Future, Civic Vitality report, Houstonians consider themselves 27% democrat, 21% republican and 25% don’t identify with any established political party.

Houston, Houston, Do you Read Me?

Houston is home to the NASA Astronaut Corps, the Lyndon B. Johnson Space Center .

This is where astronauts get the training to go into space and Mission Control for when they are in space. We literally produce people out of this world!

Family and Community Oriented.

According to the same Civic Vitality Report study, two thirds of Houstonians have dinner with their family every day and 47% get together with their family on a daily basis.

Plus, they hold on to traditional community unity values long gone in other major cities with 60% admitting to being neighborly on a regular basis and 36% being involved in local community organizations. Won’t you be my neighbor?

You’re in the Money!

According to Business Insider, Houston is home to 26 of the Fortune 500 companies being 2 nd in the U.S. following New York. This city welcomes entrepreneurs with open arms. Famous Inventions

A patchwork quilt of talents, Houston has provided fertile grounds for famous inventions. Here are a few that you might not have known of:

The Underwire Bra:  Invented by Howard Hughes.

Condensed Milk:  Invented by Gail Borden Jr.

Source:  Houston Press & Chron.com

Get Your Rodeo On!

You’ve got to plan your next trip to Houston during rodeo season. The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo or HLSR, is Houston’s trademark event and one of the largest live entertainment and livestock exhibitions in the world. The celebration is kicked off with a parade and a 5K run. Besides the five star rodeo and livestock competitions, the twenty day long event includes a mouth watering BBQ cook-off, live concerts, pig racing, a carnival and a wine competition. It’s come a long way since it was first started back in 1931 when seven men met for lunch.
